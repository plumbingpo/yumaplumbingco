Warning Signs for Garbage Disposal Repair

Recognizing the warning signs early can save you time, money, and prevent significant damage to your property. Watch for these telltale signs that professional help is needed:

  • Disposal hums when turned on but the blades don't spin
  • No response at all when the switch is flipped â€” no hum, no noise
  • Persistent foul odor from the disposal despite cleaning attempts
  • Leaking from the bottom of the unit, at the mounting ring, or from drain connections
  • Disposal takes much longer to grind food waste than it used to
  • Frequent jams that require manual resetting with the Allen wrench

Maintenance Tips for Garbage Disposal Repair

A little prevention goes a long way in avoiding expensive plumbing repairs. Here are our expert recommendations:

  • Always run cold water before, during, and for 15-20 seconds after operating the disposal to flush waste through
  • Feed food waste gradually â€” don't stuff the disposal full and turn it on all at once
  • Avoid hard items like bones, fruit pits, and shellfish shells that can damage or jam the blades
  • Never put fibrous vegetables (celery, corn husks, onion skins), pasta, rice, or potato peels in the disposal
  • Clean the disposal regularly by grinding ice cubes to sharpen blades and citrus peels to deodorize
  • Never pour grease or oil into the disposal â€” it solidifies in drain pipes and causes clogs
  • Use the disposal regularly even if you don't have food waste â€” running it prevents rust and corrosion from inactivity

Garbage Disposal Repair FAQ

Garbage disposal installation in Yuma typically costs $200-$500 total, including a quality disposal unit and professional installation. A basic 1/3 HP unit runs on the lower end, while a premium 1 HP continuous-feed disposal with sound insulation runs on the higher end. We provide exact pricing before starting work.
For 1-2 person households with light use, a 1/3 HP disposal is sufficient. Families of 3-4 should consider a 1/2 to 3/4 HP unit. Larger families or heavy users benefit from a 3/4 to 1 HP disposal. Higher horsepower units are also quieter due to better insulation and grind food more finely, reducing drain clogs.
Most kitchen sinks can accommodate a garbage disposal, but the drain opening must be a standard 3.5-inch size (most are). If your sink has a different drain size, an adapter may be needed. Some very thin or lightweight sinks may require additional support for heavier disposal units. We'll assess compatibility during our visit.
Bad odors usually come from food particles trapped on the grinding components and inside the splash guard. To clean: grind a tray of ice cubes with a cup of rock salt, then grind lemon or lime halves. For persistent odors, the splash guard can be removed and scrubbed. If odors persist after cleaning, there may be a clog or buildup in the drain pipe below.
A humming disposal usually means the flywheel is jammed â€” something is stuck between the impellers and the grind ring. Try pressing the reset button on the bottom of the unit, then use the Allen wrench (usually included with the disposal) in the center hole on the bottom to manually turn the flywheel. If this doesn't work, or if the motor has overheated and burned out, we can diagnose and fix or replace it.
The average garbage disposal lasts 8-15 years depending on quality, usage, and maintenance. Higher-horsepower units from premium brands tend to last longer. Signs it's time to replace include frequent jams, leaking from the body (not connections), excessive noise, and poor grinding performance even after clearing jams.
