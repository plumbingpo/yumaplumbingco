The pipes running through your home are the arteries of your plumbing system, carrying fresh water in and waste water out every single day. Over time, pipes can corrode, develop leaks, accumulate mineral deposits, and eventually fail. When pipe problems arise, Yuma Plumbing Co provides expert pipe repair and repiping services throughout Yuma, using modern materials and proven techniques that restore your plumbing system to optimal performance.

Many homes in the Yuma area were built with galvanized steel pipes, which have a typical lifespan of 40-50 years and are prone to internal corrosion that causes low water pressure, rusty water, and eventually leaks. Other homes may have polybutylene pipes (manufactured from the 1970s-1990s) which are known to fail prematurely. If your home has these materials, proactive repiping with modern copper or PEX piping can prevent future emergencies and significantly improve your water quality and pressure.

Whether you need a single pipe section repaired, a specific area repiped, or a complete whole-house repipe, our licensed plumbers evaluate your system, explain your options clearly, and perform the work with minimal disruption to your daily life. We specialize in both copper and PEX piping systems and can recommend the best material for your specific situation, budget, and goals.

Warning Signs for Pipe Repair

Recognizing the warning signs early can save you time, money, and prevent significant damage to your property. Watch for these telltale signs that professional help is needed:

  • Rusty, brown, or discolored water when you first turn on faucets
  • Noticeably low water pressure throughout the house or in specific areas
  • Frequent pipe leaks requiring repeated repairs
  • Visible corrosion, green staining, or flaking on exposed pipes
  • Pinhole leaks appearing in copper pipes, especially in hot water lines
  • Your home has galvanized steel, polybutylene, or lead pipes
  • Water has a metallic taste or leaves stains on fixtures and laundry

The Benefits of Professional Pipe Repair

Pipe repair and especially repiping is complex work that requires a thorough understanding of plumbing system design, pipe sizing calculations, proper support and anchoring, soldering or connection techniques, and building code requirements. In Arizona, repiping work requires licensed plumbers and typically needs a permit and inspection.

A professional repipe also involves strategic planning to minimize wall and ceiling openings, proper insulation of hot water lines, correct pipe routing for efficient pressure and flow, and coordination of water shutoffs to minimize inconvenience. Our experienced plumbers have completed hundreds of repipe projects and know how to complete the job efficiently while keeping your home clean and your water downtime to a minimum.

How to Prevent Pipe Repair Problems

A little prevention goes a long way in avoiding expensive plumbing repairs. Here are our expert recommendations:

  • Monitor water pressure regularly â€” sudden drops may indicate a developing leak or corroding pipes
  • Watch for signs of corrosion on visible pipes, especially at joints and connections
  • If you have hard water, consider a water softener to reduce mineral buildup inside pipes
  • Maintain consistent home heating in winter to prevent pipe freezing, especially in exterior walls
  • Don't use chemical drain cleaners â€” they can corrode pipes from the inside over time
  • Have your water tested if you notice changes in taste, color, or odor â€” this may indicate pipe deterioration
  • If your home is over 40 years old, schedule a plumbing inspection to assess pipe condition and remaining lifespan
