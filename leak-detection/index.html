Water leaks are one of the most insidious plumbing problems a homeowner can face. While a dripping faucet is obvious and annoying, hidden leaks behind walls, under slabs, and underground can go undetected for weeks or months, silently causing structural damage, mold growth, and inflated water bills. According to the EPA, the average household's leaks waste approximately 10,000 gallons of water per year â€” and some leaks waste far more.

Yuma Plumbing Co uses advanced, non-invasive leak detection technology to pinpoint the exact location of hidden leaks without unnecessary demolition. Our arsenal includes acoustic listening devices that amplify the sound of pressurized water escaping from pipes, thermal imaging cameras that detect temperature differences caused by leaking water, and electronic leak detection equipment that can locate leaks under concrete slabs with pinpoint accuracy.

Once we've located the leak, our experienced plumbers repair it using the most appropriate method for your situation â€” whether that's a targeted spot repair, pipe relining, or in some cases, a section of pipe replacement. We always aim for the least invasive repair that provides a permanent solution, minimizing disruption to your home and your life.

Warning Signs for Leak Detection

Recognizing the warning signs early can save you time, money, and prevent significant damage to your property. Watch for these telltale signs that professional help is needed:

  • Unexplained increase in your monthly water bill without changes in usage
  • Sound of running water when no fixtures are in use
  • Damp, warm, or discolored spots on floors, walls, or ceilings
  • Mold or mildew growth in unexpected areas, especially in normally dry locations
  • Musty or earthy odor in certain rooms that persists after cleaning
  • Cracks in the foundation or shifting of door and window frames
  • Water meter continues to spin when all water fixtures are turned off

Maintenance Tips for Leak Detection

A little prevention goes a long way in avoiding expensive plumbing repairs. Here are our expert recommendations:

  • Monitor your water bill each month â€” a sudden increase often indicates a hidden leak somewhere in your system
  • Check your water meter before bed and first thing in the morning without using water to detect slow leaks
  • Inspect visible pipes under sinks, in the basement, and in utility areas regularly for signs of moisture or corrosion
  • Replace washing machine supply hoses every 5 years â€” braided stainless steel hoses are more reliable than rubber
  • Maintain consistent indoor heating during winter to prevent pipes from freezing and potentially cracking
  • Install water leak detection sensors near water heaters, washing machines, dishwashers, and under sinks
  • Have your plumbing system professionally inspected every 2 years, especially if your home is over 25 years old
